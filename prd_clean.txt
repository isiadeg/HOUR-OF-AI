1. Executive Summary
The Nurture Roots Hour of AI Platform is a web-based volunteer orchestration and learning coordination platform designed to support a global AI learning programme for children and young people.
The platform connects:
Volunteer mentors (primarily Microsoft employees and global professionals)
Remote learners worldwide
Learners attending physical community learning hubs in underserved regions
The system automates learner and mentor registration, class allocation, volunteer scheduling, session coordination, impact tracking, and volunteer hours reporting.
The platform will support both online learners and physical hubs while ensuring structured mentor coverage, reliable session delivery, and scalable programme operations.
2. Background
Nurture Roots Foundation currently runs the Hour of AI, a weekly global learning initiative focused on introducing children and young people to Artificial Intelligence, coding, and digital literacy.
Sessions are delivered virtually through:
Microsoft Teams
Code.org learning resources
The programme operates using volunteer mentors and supports learners from diverse global locations, including underserved rural communities through physical learning hubs.
Current coordination activities are largely manual, creating challenges in:
Scheduling
Availability matching
Session management
Volunteer coordination
Impact reporting
Mentor replacement handling
The platform aims to solve these operational challenges and enable global scaling.
3. Product Vision
To build a scalable global platform that enables:
Efficient coordination of volunteer-led AI education
Automated learner and mentor matching
Reliable delivery of weekly sessions
Real-time impact measurement
Seamless support for both remote learners and physical hubs
4. Product Objectives
The platform objectives are to:
Automate mentor and learner registration
Match mentors and learners automatically
Reduce manual operational overhead
Ensure reliable mentor coverage for every class
Track volunteer hours and programme impact
Support underserved communities through physical hubs
Improve learner retention and engagement
Enable future scalability across multiple programmes
